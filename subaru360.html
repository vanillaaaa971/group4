Information:

The Subaru 360 is a rear-engined, two-door city car manufactured and marketed from 1958 to 1971 by Subaru. As the company's first automobile, production reached 392,000 over its 12-year model run.

Description:

The Subaru 360 is a charming and iconic microcar produced by Subaru in the 1960s. Known for its compact size and quirky design, it was one of Subaru's first mass-produced vehicles. Despite its small stature, the 360 offered practicality and affordability, making it popular in crowded urban areas. With its distinctive appearance and efficient engine, the Subaru 360 holds a special place in automotive history as a symbol of Japan's post-war economic boom and the country's burgeoning automotive industry.
