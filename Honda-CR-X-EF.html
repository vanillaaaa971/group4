Information:

The Honda CR-X, also known as the Honda Ballade Sports CR-X in Japan, is a front-wheel-drive sport compact car produced by Honda from 1983 to 1991. With nearly 400,000 units manufactured, it gained popularity for its performance and fuel efficiency. In the U.S., it was marketed as an economy sport Kammback with seating for two, while models in other regions offered a 2+2 arrangement. The CR-X was eventually succeeded by Honda's CR-X del Sol.
