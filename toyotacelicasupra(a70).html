Description:

The Toyota Celica Supra (A60, A70) is a series of sports coupes praised for their powerful engines, sleek design, and engaging driving dynamics.

Information:

Toyota redesigned the Celica Supra in 1981, known as the Celica XX in Japan. Key differences included a longer wheelbase, retractable headlights, and a six-cylinder engine. The front suspension used Macpherson struts while the rear used a semi-trailing link design. Japanese buyers had a liftback option called the Soarer coupe, while the performance saloon called the Celica Camry was realigned with the Carina.
