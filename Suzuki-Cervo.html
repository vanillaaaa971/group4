Information:

Suzuki pioneered the kei car in 1955. In 1971, they launched the Fronte Coupé, a compact GT car. It had a 2+2 seating layout and was powered by a 359cc two-stroke engine. Discontinued in 1976 due to regulations, Suzuki returned in 1977 with the Cervo SS20. It had a 539cc engine and retained the Fronte Coupé's design with practical modifications like a rear glass hatch for cargo access.

Description:

The Suzuki Cervo is a compact car produced by Suzuki, primarily for the Japanese domestic market. Known for its small size and fuel efficiency, the Cervo offers practical transportation in urban environments. It features a distinctive retro design and is available in both hatchback and coupe body styles. Despite its compact dimensions, the Cervo provides a surprisingly spacious interior and comfortable driving experience. With its affordability and maneuverability, the Suzuki Cervo is a popular choice for city dwellers and those seeking an economical vehicle
