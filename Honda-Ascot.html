Information:

The Honda Ascot is a compact sedan made by Honda, sold exclusively in Japan from 1989 to 1997. The first generation included two versions: the Ascot and the Ascot Innova, a "pillared hardtop" model. The Innova shared mechanics with the European-market Accord. The second generation, shared with the Honda Rafaga, improved the platform. The name "Ascot" was chosen for its association with elegance, referencing the Ascot Racecourse and Ascot tie. Honda previously used the Ascot name for a range of motorcycles in the 1980s.

Description:

The Honda Ascot is a mid-size sedan produced by Honda, known for its comfortable ride and reliable performance. It was manufactured from 1989 to 1997 and was sold primarily in Japan. The Ascot offered a spacious interior, modern features, and efficient engines, making it a popular choice for families and commuters. With its blend of comfort and practicality, the Honda Ascot provided a smooth and enjoyable driving experience for its owners
